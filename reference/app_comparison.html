Deep Dive

Each app, in detail.

On-boat capability, post-race analysis, and the honest verdict for each option.

01
"Trim your sails, not your data."
MakerSailLab Analytics
OriginDenmark
StatusOpen Beta
CostFree
Visit saillab.app
View app screenshots →

The closest thing on the market to a spiritual successor to RaceQs — built around the radical idea that you shouldn't have to coordinate with anyone to get fleet comparison.

On the Boat
  • In-app race tracker with start timer and line-end "ping" feature for capturing the start line
  • Phone GPS recording with offline support — uploads when you reach connectivity
  • No live tactical display (not its purpose — recording is passive in the background)
  • Imports tracks from a Vakaros Atlas (VKX) if you've recorded with one instead
After the Finish
  • Automatic race detection — finds your races and upwind/downwind legs without setup
  • Leg-by-leg splits across the whole fleet, no manual stopwatching
  • Tacking-speed comparison: time lost vs. fleet median, per maneuver
  • Start-line analysis: position, speed, and time-to-line vs. the front row
  • VMG comparison upwind and downwind against fleet average — not just one other boat
  • Mobile-only views (no web portal for projecting at the club bar)
The honest take The cleanest auto-detection of any app here, free during beta, and the only one whose fleet model actually works without coordination. The tradeoff is that the magic only fires if other boats in your fleet are also recording — and there's no web interface, so debriefs run from a phone screen.
02
"Record, replay, and analyze your races."
MakerFirstByte
PlatformiOS only
ConnectivityCellular required
CostFree + paid
Visit sailtrace.app
View app screenshots →

A clean, modern iOS race tracker with a strong analytics layer behind a paywall. Spiritually the most direct RaceQs replacement on the iPhone, but locked to one platform.

On the Boat
  • Hyper-accurate phone GPS that runs in the background with the screen locked
  • Requires a cellular data connection — important if you race offshore
  • Battery-optimized for hours of recording on a charged phone
  • No external hardware support documented
After the Finish
  • Dynamic race replays with the ability to scrub timeline
  • Fleet comparison with synchronized replays of every boat in the same race
  • Color-coded speed maps to see where you were fast or slow on the course
  • Automatic detection of tacks, gybes, and mark roundings with exact locations
  • Layline analysis — see how much you overstood or understood each layline
  • Clear-air statistics (advanced metrics live behind the paid subscription)
  • Stats viewable on both phone and computer; works for one-design and PHRF fleets
The honest take Polished and focused. If your fleet is all-iPhone, this is potentially the best-feeling product in the lineup. The catch: Android sailors are out, and the genuinely interesting analytics (clear air, layline efficiency, deeper metrics) sit behind a subscription.
03
"More than just a tracker."
MakerMWM
Users313,000+
Sports30+ water sports
TiersFree · Pro · Ultra
Visit waterspeedapp.com
View app screenshots →

The most hardware-friendly app in the field, and the broadest in scope — Waterspeed covers sailing alongside windsurfing, wing-foiling, SUP, and dozens of other water sports. A real web app makes it the most "debrief at a laptop" friendly option short of SailSync.

On the Boat
  • Live speed, VMG, and heart rate visible on Apple Watch or Garmin without the phone
  • Race countdown timers and start-line tools built into the app and watch face
  • Integrates with Apple Watch, Garmin, Coros, Suunto — with Wear OS support coming
  • Imports tracks from a Vakaros Atlas Edge or Atlas 2 after the session
  • Built-in safety features including live location sharing while offshore
After the Finish
  • The new "Ultra" tier breaks sessions into upwind and downwind legs automatically
  • VMG analysis, polar charts, tack and gybe counts, foiling efficiency
  • Compare sessions over time — trend analysis across days, weeks, equipment changes
  • Personal-record tracking for short bursts and long runs
  • Web App is the primary upload destination for Vakaros files and the best debrief surface
  • Less fleet-aggregation focused than SailLab or SailSync — more about your own progress
The honest take The depth-to-cost ratio is best if you're a solo performance sailor or do multiple water sports. Hardware coverage is unmatched here. The weakness for your use case: it doesn't really do fleet comparison the way SailLab or SailTrace do — it's a "you vs. your past self" tool more than a "you vs. the fleet" tool.
04
"Sailing insights made easy."
MakerSail Sync, LLC
OriginSacramento, CA
WebFull platform
Free tier3 boats/session
Visit sailsync.ai
View app screenshots →

A two-piece system: iOS app for capture plus a serious web platform for analysis. Built around the coach-led debrief workflow, but the free tier is generous enough for small training groups even without a coach.

On the Boat
  • Live group tracking with shared start countdowns synchronized across devices
  • Live map showing mark positions and other boats in your session
  • Distance to the starting line displayed in real time
  • Native iOS recording, plus direct Garmin watch integration (auto-syncs after session)
  • Accepts Vakaros, Sailmon, Velocitek tracks; also imports GPX and Strava activities
  • Video recording from your phone for later overlay on the track
After the Finish
  • FLOAssist — AI-powered debrief reports that turn the day's data into a written summary
  • Telemetry video replays — overlay speed, VMG, and position on top of footage
  • Dedicated race start analysis: timing, speed, positioning at the gun
  • Per-leg and per-maneuver breakdowns with side-by-side comparisons
  • Find target speeds across different setups and tuning configurations
  • PDF report exports for sharing with crew or athletes
  • One upload link gathers all sailors' tracks into a single session — no chasing files
The honest take The most complete debrief platform in this comparison. If your debrief workflow involves projecting at the club or sharing structured feedback, nothing else is close. The constraint is the 3-boat free ceiling — perfect for training partners, painful for a full club race. Paid tiers ($21–$63/month) are genuine money for casual use.
05
"Milliseconds matter."
MakerSailRacer.net
OriginLithuania
HardwareInkDisplay (opt.)
Premium€35/year
Visit sailracer.net
View app screenshots →

The most explicitly tactical app in this lineup — built around the moments before and during a race, not the debrief afterward. Strongest when paired with the proprietary InkDisplay hardware. The post-race analysis side is solid but secondary.

On the Boat
  • Live tactical decisions: lay lines, polar speed targets, wind shifts, current effect
  • Precise start timing — calculates the favored end and time-to-line at full speed
  • Distance and bearing to next mark, adjusted for current
  • Optional InkDisplay hardware — high-contrast e-paper screen, visible through polarized sunglasses, waterproof
  • Watch app available for Garmin (and previously for Pebble — yes, that Pebble)
  • Strong club-event integration via the SailRacer.org event side
After the Finish
  • Race replay mode showing where the race was won and lost
  • Leg-by-leg analysis: speeds, times, tacking angles, and VMG comparisons
  • Distance lost during tacks and gybes — measured against TWD or COG axis
  • Time-of-maneuver and distance-lost-to-mark metrics
  • Progressive series analysis (more useful at organized events than informal racing)
  • Web-based race viewer for event organizers — works on any device
The honest take Different category than the others — it's a live tactical tool first, with post-race as a secondary feature. If you want a sailing computer in your cockpit, especially with the InkDisplay, this is the only entry here that fits. If your priority is debrief on the bar TV after racing, the others serve that better.
Final Recommendation

Match the app to the situation.

No single answer wins. Below — five common scenarios and the pick for each.

Scenario 01
"I want RaceQs-style fleet replay, totally free, with no setup."
SailLab
Auto race detection, fleet aggregation by time and location, no event codes. Only catch — fellow sailors need to be running it too, and you'll be debriefing on a phone screen.
Scenario 02
"My training partners and I want serious post-race debriefs."
SailSync
The 3-boat free tier is built for exactly this. Full web platform, AI debrief reports, video overlay, broad hardware support. The most "professional debrief" feel of any free option.
Scenario 03
"I want a live tactical computer in my cockpit during racing."
SailRacer
The only entry built primarily for live tactical decision-making. The InkDisplay hardware is genuinely best-in-class for sunlight visibility — but adds cost.
Scenario 04
"I do multiple water sports and care about my own performance."
Waterspeed
Broadest hardware support, real web app, and analytics that travel across sailing, foiling, windsurfing, SUP, and more. Less about peer comparison; more about your trajectory over time.
Scenario 05
"My whole fleet is on iPhone and we want clean race replays."
SailTrace
Polished, focused, and the cleanest synchronized-replay experience if everyone's on iOS. Just be aware that the deeper analytics (clear-air, layline efficiency) live behind a paywall.
Pragmatic Combo
"What if I want it all?"
SailLab + SailSync free
Record once on your phone with SailLab for the auto-detected fleet view. Upload the same track to SailSync to use the AI debrief and video overlay for your own boat. Zero cost, two complementary lenses.
